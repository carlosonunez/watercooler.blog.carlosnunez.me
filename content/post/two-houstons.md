- Now
  - Quality of life is amazing
  - Walkability is off the charts
      - Two breweries within ten minutes by foot
      - Two powerlifting gyms, one less then ten minutes by foot; the other, 15
      - We care a lot about food; many restaurants within 30 minutes away by foot
  - Bus train etc.
    - Everything else that I can't walk to is a bus or light rail ride away
    - Whatever is too far by bus can be accessed by Waymo
    - My car these days is only used for road trips and picking up take-out
      (FUCK DOORDASH)
      - I'm strongly thinking of getting an e-bike to steal take-out retrieving
        duties from the car
- It wasn't always like this
    - Suburb, three years ago
    - Walkability was off the charts, but in the other direction
        - Starbucks was our nearest coffee shop, and it was a two mile walk
          round-trip
        - The nearest gym was slightly closer, but I had to walk on streets the
          entire way
            - One of those streets had a 60mph speed limit!!!
    - Motordom
        - The car was king, queen and the entire royal court
        - Roads in the town all fed into two arterial roads; one of which was
          a single lane in each direction
            - Road rage was a daily event, no matter how strongly I focussed on
              my breath
            - This motivated me to get GoPros for my bike and my helmet,
              something I never felt compelled to do before
- Two experiences, one Houston
    - I didn't move to NYC, LA or Chicago
    - I didn't live in some generic modern suburb
    - Both of these experiences were in Houston!
- YMMV
    - Houston has something for everyone at reasonable prices
    - People that love the suburban lifestyle (for reasons I can't understand)
      have just as many options as people who prefer walkable cities and the
      amenities that they provide
- The catch? Weather
    - Unless you love hot weather, like I do, which, in that case, the weather
      isn't a catch at all
    - Regardless, most people prefer moderate climate, which Houston has outside
      of brutal summer months
